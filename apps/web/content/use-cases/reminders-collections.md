## The Reminder and Collections Problem

Two categories of calls cause massive operational inefficiency for businesses that rely on them: reminder calls (appointment, medication, payment due) and collections calls (past-due invoices, delinquent accounts, late fees).

**Reminders** are high-volume, low-complexity calls that a business must make proactively to prevent missed appointments, missed doses, or late payments. Most businesses don't make them consistently because the manual effort is too high.

**Collections** are sensitive, compliance-regulated calls that require consistency, documentation, and volume. Human collectors are expensive, prone to inconsistency, and create compliance risk. AI is inherently consistent and auditable.

## Reminder Use Cases

### Payment Due Reminders

Before a payment is due — not after it's late — AI agents call customers:
- "Hi, this is a reminder that your payment of $847.50 for your account with [Company] is due on Friday the 15th. Would you like to make a payment now, or set up automatic payments?"
- Accept payment over the phone (PCI-compliant)
- Set up autopay enrollment
- Schedule a payment arrangement for customers who need more time

Sending proactive reminders before due dates reduces late payments by 40–60% compared to waiting and collecting after.

### Appointment Reminders (Cross-Industry)
Healthcare, dental, salon, legal, home services — AI appointment reminder calls with a reschedule option reduce no-shows by 40–65%.

### Medication Adherence Reminders
For pharmacies, health plans, and clinical programs, AI agents call patients to remind them to take medications, refill prescriptions, and attend monitoring appointments.

### Subscription Renewal Reminders
SaaS, insurance, and subscription services use AI agents to call before renewal dates:
- Alert customers of upcoming renewal
- Confirm payment method is current
- Offer renewal incentives
- Process credit card updates

## Collections Use Cases

### Early-Stage Delinquency (1–30 Days Past Due)

The most productive collections window. Accounts are recent, contact rates are higher, and customers are more likely to resolve. AI agents:
- Call 3, 7, 10, 15, 20, and 30 days past due
- Provide a clear, non-threatening reminder of the overdue balance
- Offer payment options (full payment, payment plan, partial payment)
- Accept payment immediately via IVR or transfer to billing

### Mid-Stage Collections (30–90 Days Past Due)

- More direct language about consequences (late fees, service interruption, credit reporting)
- Offer structured payment plans
- Escalate unresolved accounts to human collectors for negotiation

### Late-Stage and Pre-Charge-Off (90–180 Days)

- Final settlement offers before account charge-off
- Transfer to third-party collections with documentation
- Document all contact attempts for legal purposes

### Healthcare Revenue Cycle

Medical billing collections is one of the most challenging collection environments — patient sensitivity, insurance complexity, financial hardship considerations. AI agents are designed for compassionate collection:
- Reference specific services and dates
- Explain insurance coordination and patient responsibility
- Offer financial assistance programs
- Set up payment plans without judgment

## Compliance Architecture for Collections AI

Collections is one of the most heavily regulated communication types. Every outbound collections call must comply with FDCPA, TCPA, and applicable state laws.

**FDCPA Requirements (QuickVoice Built-In):**
- Mini-Miranda disclosure on every call: "This is an attempt to collect a debt, and any information obtained will be used for that purpose."
- No calls before 8 AM or after 9 PM in debtor's time zone
- No harassing, abusive, or threatening language
- Cease and desist handling — when a debtor requests, calls stop immediately and the request is logged
- Verification of debt handling when disputed
- No communication with third parties about the debt

**TCPA Requirements:**
- Prior express consent for automated calls to mobile numbers
- Do-not-call list scrubbing before every campaign
- Calling hour windows enforced by state
- Opt-out handling within calls

**Documentation and Audit Trail:**
Every QuickVoice collections call is:
- Logged with timestamp, call disposition, and recording
- Searchable for regulatory audit
- Exportable for compliance reporting

## Best Practices for AI Reminder and Collections Calls

1. **Call at the right time.** 10 AM–12 PM and 2–5 PM local time achieve the highest connection rates. Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons.

2. **Keep reminders positive.** For pre-due date reminders, the tone should be helpful and service-oriented — not threatening.

3. **Offer a solution, not just a demand.** "Would you like to make a payment now, or would a payment plan work better?" is more effective than "Your account is overdue."

4. **Make it easy to pay.** Accept payment within the call. Transfer to a payment portal with a 4-digit PIN. Send a payment link by SMS. Every barrier to payment reduces recovery.

5. **Escalate appropriately.** AI handles high-volume, low-complexity collections. Human collectors handle complex disputes, high-value accounts, and sensitive situations.
